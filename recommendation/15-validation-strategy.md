# Validation Strategy
## How to test this assessment against reality: starting week one

*An external assessment is a set of hypotheses, not a plan. This is the order in which I would try to falsify my own conclusions.*

---

## Week 1–2: internal data pulls that could change the ranking

| Question | Data source | Could invalidate |
|---|---|---|
| What is Premium churn/renewal since the January increase? | Billing | The urgency (or sufficiency) of the flagship |
| What did the acknowledged at-risk-warning fix already scope? | Jira / the owning team | Phase 1 scope (join vs. lead) |
| Does capture/fill telemetry exist? At what fidelity? | Client analytics owners | Brief 1's measurement plan |
| Why has #1620 (save prompt) survived five years? | Extension team history | Whether brief 1's top item is priority or architecture |
| Root-cause split of the 50 passkey issues | Mobile team triage | Brief 5's entire leverage estimate |
| Collections-survey findings | Research team | Brief 3 sequencing |
| Real support-ticket taxonomy volumes | Support/CS | Deflection sizing across briefs |

## Weeks 2–6: qualitative validation

- **8–10 moderated sessions with Enhanced Premium subscribers** (mix: loyalty-discount steppers, new full-price, long-tenured): what do the warnings mean to you, what did Premium do for you this year, what would make renewal obvious? Tests the flagship's value-attribution assumptions before instrumenting them.
- **5 sessions with recent importers** (large vaults): observe the first organization attempt. Tests brief 3's post-import hypothesis.
- **Community listening with structure:** re-read the top warning threads *after* Phase 1 scoping, tag by requested control type, dismissal, snooze, explanation, threshold tuning, to rank Phase 1 sub-features by demand rather than intuition.
- **Sales/CS interviews (3–4):** where does Enhanced Premium value land or fail in renewal conversations; what do enterprise buyers ask about posture explanations? Tests whether the transparency property has enterprise pull (vision Horizon 3).

## Weeks 4–10: the community RFC as validation instrument

The methodology RFC (flagship Phase 2) is itself the largest validation event: it tests, in public, whether "auditable guidance" is a story the community amplifies or shrugs at. Design it to yield signal: a real comment window, named maintainer responses, a published disposition table (proposal → adopted/deferred/declined + reason). Success threshold pre-registered: substantive participation, net-positive sentiment, ≥1 adopted community proposal.

## Standing falsification discipline

- Every brief carries pre-registered hypotheses (H1–H3) and the experiments that test them (recommendation/16). No hypothesis, no build.
- Every readout states its measurement tier (metrics/14 degraded path) and confidence honestly.
- Quarterly: re-rank the opportunity comparison with accumulated internal evidence. If brief 5's taxonomy shows 80% upstream fault, its rank falls and the advocacy strategy replaces the product strategy, the assessment is designed to be revised, because that is the property it is trying to demonstrate.
