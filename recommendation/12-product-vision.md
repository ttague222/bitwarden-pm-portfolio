# Product Vision
## The security company that shows its work: all the way into the product

*Revised from my v1 vision after the research. The v1 version imagined adding a guided layer to a silent toolbox. The evidence corrected the premise: Bitwarden already started guiding (vault health, Dec 2025), and stumbled on exactly the values its community holds. The vision, restated on evidence:*

---

## Vision statement

> **Bitwarden guides every user toward measurably stronger security, with reasoning the user can read, controls the user can exercise, and a methodology anyone can audit, because a security company's guidance should be as transparent as its code.**

## Why this vision is Bitwarden's to own

The research found one strategic asset no competitor can replicate and one community demand no competitor faces this sharply:

- **The asset:** open methodology. 1Password cannot publish Watchtower's rules; Keeper will not publish its scoring. Bitwarden publishes its cryptography and survives audits in public (ETH Zurich, Feb 2026). Extending show-your-work from code to *guidance* is the natural next chapter of the same identity.
- **The demand:** the at-risk-warnings episode. The community's top requests of the year were not "more coaching", they were *agency* (dismissal) and *reasoning* (why flagged). This user base wants to be guided the way GitHub guides developers: factually, specifically, dismissibly, with the logic on the table.

The vision therefore isn't "become a coach." It is: **be the only security product whose advice you can audit**, the property that turns the year's loudest complaint into the next decade's differentiation.

## What it looks like when it's working

- Every warning, alert, or recommendation in any Bitwarden client carries three things: the reason (in plain language, per item), the control (dismiss, snooze, or act, honored permanently), and the reference (a versioned, public methodology document behind it).
- The methodology evolves in public: rules are RFC'd, changes are changelogged, community-proposed detections are a contribution surface alongside code.
- Guidance quality is measured by the mission metric, **at-risk items actually resolved per user**, never by engagement with the warnings themselves. Dismissal is respected data, not failure.
- Paying users can see what Enhanced Premium did for them: a factual annual account ("this year: 2 breach alerts, 14 at-risk items resolved, 5GB used"), not marketing.
- Enterprise inherits the same property: Access Intelligence dashboards trace every flag to published logic, posture reporting an auditor can follow.

## What the vision refuses (the refusals are the strategy)

1. Nothing sits in front of the unlock → find → fill loop. Guidance waits its turn.
2. No un-dismissible anything. Agency is not a preference; it is the product's posture toward its users.
3. No monetization dressed as security advice, the Keeper line. Paid suggestions are labeled before the tap, ranked after free actions.
4. No engagement theater: no streaks, no guilt, no urgency mechanics. The register is GitHub's security tab, not a fitness app.
5. No guidance the company can't explain publicly. If a rule can't survive an RFC, it doesn't ship.

## Three horizons

- **Horizon 1 (now → mid-2027):** repair and prove, the flagship program (recommendation/13): agency + reasons on the shipped surface, methodology published, value measured against the January 2027 renewal cohort.
- **Horizon 2:** extend guidance from warnings to *paths*, from "this password is at risk" to sequenced, evidence-ranked next actions (import cleanup, 2FA coverage, passkey upgrades), on the same transparent chassis. This is where the durable core of my v1 "Security Coach" concept re-enters, earned, this time, by a repaired foundation.
- **Horizon 3:** the transparent-guidance property becomes platform-wide: consumer clients, Admin Console, and (if the demand materializes) the agent-access line all share one published reasoning framework, one company voice: *here is what we flagged, here is why, here is the control you hold.*
