# Presentation Deck
## "What the Evidence Says: A Product Opportunity Assessment of Bitwarden"
### 16 slides · 25 minutes + discussion · speaker notes in 19-speaker-notes.md

---

**Slide 1, Title.**
What the Evidence Says: A Product Opportunity Assessment. Thomas Tague. One line of method: 64 repos, ~10,000 issues, the full forum vote record, 19 months of releases, external data only, confidence levels stated throughout.

**Slide 2, Why I did this instead of a cover letter.**
A resume says what I did. This shows how I decide. Including, especially, where the evidence made me throw away my own first idea.

**Slide 3, What Bitwarden got right (sincerely).**
Trust as a moat: open source, published audits (ETH Zurich, Feb 2026), zero-knowledge, the category's only credible free tier, the 8-day GPL relicense response. These aren't compliments; they're the assets the recommendations are built on.

**Slide 4, The strategy is visible in what shipped.**
One chart: 19-month investment allocation. Enterprise posture (~25%), passkey lifecycle, encryption v2, monetization tightening, AI-agent credentials emerging. My recommendations serve this strategy; none redirect it.

**Slide 5, Finding 1: The 2018 wishlist is still the 2026 wishlist.**
Auto-type 890 votes. Item types 674. Sorting 672. Tags 487. Duplicates 466. All from 2018. Delivery lag on top asks: 4–7 years, and it just started moving (archive, tags, Shared Unlock).

**Slide 6, Finding 2: Peak engagement is trust, not features.**
The three biggest community events ever: a redesign backlash (973 posts), release regressions (passkeys 202👍, autofill 166👍), a license controversy (international press). This community is a guardian, not an audience.

**Slide 7, Finding 3: The most instructive event of the year.**
Dec 2025: vault health coaching ships, paid, as a repricing pillar. Weeks later, the year's top two feature requests: *let me dismiss it* (51 votes), *tell me why* (40 votes). The market wrote the spec for security guidance here: agency + published reasoning.

**Slide 8, The five opportunities (ranked).**
1. Daily-path reliability · 2. Vault Health value realization · 3. The vault-organization backlog · 4. Unlock friction · 5. Passkey reliability on mobile. One line of evidence each. Full discovery briefs in the repository.

**Slide 9, The comparison, honestly scored.**
The weighted matrix. Callouts: highest leverage (#1), highest confidence (#2), highest strategic (#5), quickest win (#2 Phase 1), longest impact (#3), mission-closest (#2).

**Slide 10, The recommendation: one PM, one program.**
Vault Health Value Realization: Repair → Transparency → Measurement → Extension. Twelve months. The dated catalyst: the January 2027 renewal cohort.

**Slide 11, The signature move.**
Publish the at-risk methodology. RFC-first. "The only security advice you can audit." 1Password structurally cannot follow. The community explicitly asked. It converts the year's worst paid-feature moment into the identity story.

**Slide 12, How it's measured.**
North star: at-risk items *resolved* per MAU, the mission in numeric form. Never warning-engagement. Degraded-measurement path specified for the privacy posture. The renewal dashboard leadership watches in January.

**Slide 13, What I'd do in week one.**
Seven internal data pulls that could change my ranking (renewal baselines, the acknowledged fix's scope, the passkey taxonomy, why #1620 survived five years). The assessment is built to be revised.

**Slide 14, How the research changed my thinking.**
Before: "build a Security Coach." After: "the coach shipped in December; repair it, open it, measure it, then earn the extension." The strongest v1 idea, published methodology, survived on merit. The rest didn't, and shouldn't have.

**Slide 15, Tradeoffs I'd want this room to challenge.**
Renewal attribution is confounded. The RFC invites criticism. Dismissal could mask risk. Reliability (#1) might deserve the hire instead, here's why I concluded otherwise, and what evidence would flip me.

**Slide 16, Close.**
"Bitwarden's advantage has always been showing its work. The opportunity is to make the product's *advice* as auditable as its code, and January 2027 is the deadline that makes it urgent. I'd love to be measured against it."
