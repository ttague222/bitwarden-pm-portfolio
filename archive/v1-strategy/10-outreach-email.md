# Outreach Email

*Two variants. Both are short on purpose — the artifact does the talking. Attach the Executive Summary as PDF; link the full document set. Replace bracketed fields before sending. (Email bodies deliberately avoid em-dashes and AI-sounding cadence; they should read like Tom wrote them, because he will be sending them.)*

---

## Variant A — to the hiring manager (primary)

**Subject:** Senior PM application, plus a product strategy doc on Bitwarden mobile

Hi [Name],

I'm applying for the Senior Product Manager role, and rather than only telling you how I think, I'd rather show you.

I spent time with the Bitwarden mobile app the way I'd approach it in the seat: a structured walkthrough, a competitive read across 1Password, Dashlane, Apple, Google, Keeper, and Microsoft, and a written strategy. Here's the short version of my conclusion.

Bitwarden's mobile app is a complete security toolbox. The native rewrite made it fast, and nothing important is missing. But most of its power depends on users discovering and configuring features themselves, and guidance ends when onboarding does. The opportunity I see is a progression layer, not new features: a Security Coach that continues after onboarding and always answers three questions. What have I completed? What should I do next? How much safer am I becoming? It runs on an on-device score whose methodology Bitwarden publishes. The first security score users can audit, from the company whose whole brand is showing its work.

The attached one-pager summarizes it. The full set includes the friction analysis, competitive teardown, a Now/Next/Later roadmap with engineering sizing, a metrics framework built to survive a minimal-telemetry posture, and the tradeoffs I'd want challenged, including where I could simply be wrong without internal data.

I'd welcome 20 minutes to hear where this matches or collides with what your team already knows.

Thanks for considering,
Thomas Tague

## Sending notes

- Keep the email under 250 words; never restate the whole thesis — the exec summary exists for that.
- The "where I could be wrong" line is deliberate: it signals the difference between a candidate selling an idea and a PM inviting evidence.
- If sending via LinkedIn InMail, use Variant B and drop the attachment reference (link only).
- Follow-up cadence: one polite follow-up after 7–9 days, then stop.
- Voice check before sending: no em-dashes in the email body (Tom's rule), contractions kept, one idea per paragraph.
