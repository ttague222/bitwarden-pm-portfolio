# Product Vision
## The Guided Security Companion

---

## Vision statement

> **Every Bitwarden user always knows what they've done to protect themselves, what to do next, and how much safer they're becoming — from a product that shows its work.**

Today, Bitwarden is the most trustworthy *toolbox* in the category. The vision is for it to become the most trustworthy *companion*: the product that doesn't just store your credentials but progressively, patiently, and transparently raises your actual security posture over months and years — without ever manipulating you into it.

## Why this vision, for this company

Most engagement strategies borrow tactics from consumer apps and hope the brand survives. This vision works *because of* Bitwarden's identity, not despite it:

- **Open source → auditable guidance.** A recommendation engine whose rules are public and a score whose methodology is published is something no competitor can copy without changing their business model. Trust becomes a product surface, not just a marketing claim.
- **Mission alignment removes the usual conflict.** For most products, "engagement" and "user benefit" diverge somewhere. Here the engagement metric (protective actions completed, credential debt reduced) *is* the mission metric. The team never has to choose.
- **The free tier makes guidance the business model.** Bitwarden's funnel is: free users → activated users → advocates and Premium/Families/Enterprise. The Coach is the machinery of the middle step — the one currently left to chance.

## The user promise (in the product's voice)

- "We'll never tell you you're *safe*. We'll show you, factually, how you're getting *stronger*."
- "Every suggestion tells you why, how long it takes, and what it's worth. You can dismiss any of them, forever."
- "The scoring runs on your device. We never see it. And you can read exactly how it works — the methodology is public, like everything else we build."

## Three horizons

**Horizon 1 — Recover the funnel (0–3 months).**
Autofill activation recovery + a simple post-onboarding checklist. Success looks like: the deferred-autofill population shrinks measurably; the checklist proves users will engage with guidance at all.

**Horizon 2 — The Coach (3–9 months).**
On-device Security Score with published methodology, recommendation queue, progress history, contextual triggers, contextual Premium moments. Success looks like: score-visible users fix more credential debt and retain better than the holdout; the community RFC on the scoring model produces engagement, not backlash; tech press writes "the open-source security score."

**Horizon 3 — The companion everywhere (9–18+ months).**
Breach-driven coaching, passkey migration guidance, Coach state synced (encrypted) across mobile/extension/desktop, family coaching surfaces, and aggregate enterprise hygiene visibility. Success looks like: the Coach is cited in enterprise deals; Families adoption grows because the product helps the family's least technical member, not just its administrator.

## What this vision refuses

Guardrails stated as product law, because for a security brand the *refusals* are the strategy:

1. Nothing ever sits in front of the unlock → find → fill loop.
2. No guilt, no streak-loss anxiety, no manufactured urgency, no fake scarcity.
3. No recommendation whose primary beneficiary is Bitwarden rather than the user; Premium suggestions are labeled, ranked below free actions, and never dressed as security warnings.
4. No posture data leaves the device unencrypted; no individual score is ever visible to an employer.
5. Everything dismissible; dismissal is honored permanently.

## How we'd know the vision is working (leading indicators)

- Users open Bitwarden for a reason other than retrieving a credential.
- Support tickets about setup fall while activation rises — guidance moved from reactive (support) to proactive (product).
- Community sentiment treats the Coach as *more* evidence of Bitwarden's values, not less.
- The phrase "am I okay?" has an answer in the product.
