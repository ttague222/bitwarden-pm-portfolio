## The decision in one view

| Question | Answer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Biggest product lever | **#1 Daily-path reliability** | It touches activation, retention, trust, support, and every feature that rides the fill path |
| Best first charter for a new Senior PM | **#2 Vault Health value realization** | Clean ownership, dated renewal catalyst, strong evidence, low crypto entanglement |
| Largest long-term goodwill reservoir | **#3 Vault organization** | Eight years of votes, import strategy creates more messy vaults, data-loss risk requires careful PM sequencing |
| Most constrained opportunity | **#4 Unlock friction** | High frequency, but tied to encryption/key-material work and migration safety |

## Why #1 does not win the hiring decision

Daily-path reliability is the highest-leverage opportunity in the portfolio. It is also the wrong place to put a newly hired Senior PM as the primary owner.

The problem crosses extension capture heuristics, mobile autofill frameworks, passkey compatibility, release engineering, QA coverage, platform-vendor constraints, and public issue management. A new PM can help by defining the measurement gap, especially save-prompt display-to-accept and regression escapes per train. But the program needs existing platform/autofill ownership plus executive sponsorship. Otherwise the new PM "owns" the most important problem while controlling too few levers.

That is the key distinction in this assessment: **highest leverage is not the same as best charter.**

## Why #2 wins

Vault Health value realization combines five properties none of the other opportunities share:

1. **The customer spec is already written.** Dismiss warnings; explain the reason.
2. **The business clock is real.** Enhanced Premium repriced in January 2026; the first full renewal read arrives around January 2027.
3. **The ownership boundary is clean.** The surface exists, the fix is acknowledged, and the next phases are product/design/community/measurement work rather than crypto migration.
4. **The differentiation is Bitwarden-native.** Publishing the at-risk methodology extends the company's "show your work" identity into product guidance.
5. **The success metric is the mission.** At-risk items actually resolved per paying user is not engagement theater; it is "stay safe online" made measurable.

## What I am choosing not to optimize for

- **Not raw demand volume.** If that were the only criterion, organization and auto-type would dominate. But some of that work is already in flight, and the riskiest parts require existing item-model ownership.
- **Not strategic glamour.** Passkeys and AI-agent access are more future-facing. Public evidence says passkey reliability needs taxonomy before productization; AI-agent credential access needs demand evidence before it deserves a top-five charter.
- **Not easy wins alone.** Dismissal and explanation are quick, but the recommendation is not "ship two settings." The real program is repair → transparency → measurement → earned extension.

## What could change the ranking

| Internal fact | Likely effect |
|---|---|
| Enhanced Premium renewal is already strong and warning complaints are support-noise only | #2 drops; #1 likely becomes the better charter |
| The autofill team lacks PM ownership and wants a dedicated lead | #1 could become the right first assignment despite cross-team scope |
| The acknowledged warning fix already includes dismissal, reasons, methodology, and renewal instrumentation | #2 narrows to measurement/extension or drops |
| Passkey taxonomy shows most failures are Bitwarden-fixable | #5 rises sharply |
| Collections survey reveals organization work is blocked on a near-term re-architecture | #3 becomes a strategic program, not a backlog delivery track |

## Recommendation

Assign the new Senior PM to **#2 Vault Health Value Realization** and give them explicit portfolio obligations:

- Hand the extension team a capture-funnel instrumentation spec for #1.
- Hand the item-model team a public narration pattern for #3.
- Hand the passkeys PM a root-cause taxonomy brief for #5.

That creates useful leverage across the portfolio without pretending one new PM can own everything.
