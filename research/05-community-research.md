## The demand structure

- **The all-time leaderboard is a 2018 time capsule.** Top unshipped asks: desktop auto-type (890 votes, 210k views, in progress), additional item types (674), sorting options (672), tags (487, staff: "the team is currently working on support for tags"), duplicate removal/merge (466), view attachments without downloading (405), persist unsaved data (339), shared unlock state (324, "actively researching," with a Shared Unlock item appearing in Jun 2026 releases), drag-and-drop (314).
- **Bitwarden does ship the leaderboard, on a 4–7 year lag.** Inline autofill, re-prompt, ssh-agent, account switching, emergency access, archive (2019→Mar 2026), passkey unlock (2020→Feb 2026) all shipped. The community has learned that voting eventually works; "eventually" is the grievance.
- **Mobile demand is parity and ergonomics, not headline features:** identities/cards autofill (96 votes, 2018), iOS save/update prompts (77), full mobile search (46), persistent vault filter (62).
- **Enterprise demand is structurally invisible on the forum** (`plans-enterprise`: 10 topics, max 4 votes), it flows through sales/CS. The loud org-adjacent asks are item-lifecycle: sharing without collections (261), unshare (282, explicitly not-planned on the ownership model), admin session visibility (235), nested-collection permission inheritance (127, unresolved since 2018). A June 2026 official survey shows collections architecture under internal re-examination.

## The engagement structure (what actually spikes)

1. **Redesign backlash is the biggest engagement event in forum history:** Dec 2024 extension redesign megathread, 973 posts, 2,264 likes, repeated at smaller scale for mobile 2025 and desktop 2026. Bitwarden's pattern: absorb via options (compact mode), never roll back.
2. **Regression storms:** Windows Hello unlock broken, 215 posts, 17,896 views.
3. **The at-risk warnings episode (the year's defining product-feedback event):** after vault health coaching shipped (Dec 2025, Premium), the top two feature requests of the period became *dismiss the warnings* (51 votes) and *explain why flagged* (40 votes), plus a 13,291-view confusion thread. Staff acknowledged: dismissal + more context is coming. **The community pre-registered the UX spec for security guidance: agency and published reasoning.**

## What I take from it

The forum is best read as a governance record, not a wishlist: stable demand, spiky trust-guardianship, and a demonstrated willingness to co-build when invited (community-maintained passkey allow-list; collections survey participation). Product motions that give the community real input compound; motions that surprise it get megathreads.
